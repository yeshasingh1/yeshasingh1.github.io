Step 1:

1.Which existing websites out there most inspired your web site design thus far?

A) http://www.studioartoholic.com/

This website uses both positive and negative space in an effective way as it showcases how you can have multiple stories/elements arranged on a page without taking up too much space. I like that the design still sits in a grid format but shows variation by having different sized a imagery/elements which aesthetically looks pleasing.

B) http://www.monsterchildren.com/

This website uses size in a great way by having the primary content quite large at the top and secondary content in a simple, clean, grid like format below which makes it a user friendly and engaging experience. This site shows great use of hierarchy of information.

2.Which parts of the design are you most proud of?

I’m most proud of the simplicity of the design so far. My aim was to create a website which was quite minimal, clean and user-friendly that allows the content to be the focus. I am still yet to add content to my website.

3.What struggles did you encounter in your design process?

My struggles so far during my process has been ensuring my design has a user-friendly mobile experience. I still haven’t nailed the design of how my website should look on mobile as I understand it’s importance as most people these days use mobiles to browse the internet. My other struggle was choosing the right font which suits the aesthetic I’m after but this was easily solved by a lot of testing and approaching friends for feedback.

4.Which struggles did you encounter during page development?

The struggle I first encountered was how to make a simple 3x3 grid which was worrying as that feature is crucial for my design. But the main struggle I encountered the most was how to make my page responsive, especially for mobile, this may be because I’m still figuring out how the overall design for mobile should be.

5.What did you learn from making your project responsive?

I learnt that designing for tablets and mobile are just as, if not, more important than designing for desktop most times. This is because users often use these items as part of their day-to-day and are most likely to view website on them. Because of this it’s very important that a website is fully functional and user-friendly for an effective journey throughout my website.

Step 2:

A) http://www.sagmeisterwalsh.com/

This website has to be one of my favourites that I have encountered so far, I love the overall layout of the website. The designers are very smart in the way they have come up with a layout that houses as much content as possible in a neat grid system, it’s very easy to navigate through. The typography is on-point, using a basic san-serif font is very easy for users to read across desktop to mobile which is very important. I do find that the navigation got a bit lost when I first landed on the website. I assumed the navigation was a ‘search’ feature at first glance due to it being boxed up the way it is. Despite that I do love that it’s a sticky navigation that follows the user as they scroll the page. The overall flow is a great experience for me, the first element I saw was their logo followed by the content which is key and because the content is so intriguing I was very engaged and loved the experience of being on the website.

B) http://www.warnerbros.com/archive/spacejam/movie/jam.htm

I found this website to be very difficult to experience. The element that I found most overwhelming was the background image, unfortunately because it’s so detailed and eye-catching it was immediately my focal point which shouldn’t be the case. The layout of the website is not an effective user experience from what I am experiencing, it also isn’t catered for mobile. The choice of a serif font isn’t a bad one but the colour choices and perhaps the size of the typography clashes with the background making the content hard to read for me. The navigation on the homepage is easy to engage with but I then got lost when I clicked on a link. It took me a while to figure out that the logo on the top left takes users back to the homepage, I feel that the navigation gets lost in the current position that it’s in. I also noticed that on some pages the navigation is then stacked on the left hand side, I feel that it should be consistent across all pages. The overall flow for the website was a difficult and overwhelming one for me, I was hard for me to gather the journey this website was taking me.
